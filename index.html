THE PROBLEM
INEFFICIENT WASTE COLLECTION
AND PROCESSING A MAJOR CAUSE OF POLLUTION(AIR AND SOIL).
EXPLANATION
The current waste management process suffers from critical inefficiencies at every stage. Initially, all types of garbage—biodegradable, plastic, metal, and hazardous waste—are indiscriminately collected into a single truck compartment, undermining the concept of source segregation. This issue is further compounded at the dumping warehouses, where waste is once again mixed without consideration for type or treatment method. As a result, the subsequent sorting process—both manual and mechanical—is not only time-consuming but also significantly delayed, creating bottlenecks in the recycling pipeline. According to a 2018 World Bank report, over 93% of waste in Asian and mid-Asian countries is collected without segregation. In India alone, less than 30% of urban local bodies have access to proper sorting and recovery facilities, as highlighted by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA). The UNEP adds to this concern by revealing that only 19% of global waste is segregated at source. This systemic inefficiency leads to substantial wastage of recyclable materials, health hazards for workers exposed to mixed waste, and a surge in overflowing landfills—posing serious environmental and public health threats.
SOLUTION
AN ELECTRIC POWERED GARBAGE SORTING TRUCK
An electrically powered powerhouse to sort and treat waste as the spot making it highly effecient.
DETAILS
The electric garbage processing truck offers a transformative solution to the waste management crisis by combining sustainability, power, and functionality. Being fully electric, it drastically reduces carbon emissions and supports the shift toward cleaner urban environments. What sets it apart is its high torque capability, allowing it to handle heavy waste loads with ease, especially on challenging terrains and during stop-start operations typical of city routes. More than just a collector, this truck is multipurpose—it incorporates waste segregation and partial processing directly at the collection point. Waste is gathered into separate compartments based on type, avoiding the common and inefficient mixing that occurs in traditional trucks and dumping warehouses. Once collected, the waste undergoes a streamlined process: initial sorting is done onboard or soon after, cutting down the days-long delay that typically happens at centralized facilities. This method tackles the three biggest problems—inefficiency in segregation, delay in recycling, and exposure of workers to harmful materials. By simplifying and accelerating the entire waste management chain, the truck reduces landfill overflow and enables cleaner, more sustainable urban living.
